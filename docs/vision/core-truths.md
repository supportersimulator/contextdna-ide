# Context DNA IDE — 5 Core Truths

These are the philosophical anchors. Everything we build must serve these. If it doesn't, it's a distraction.

## 1. Corrigibility is the highest weight

Disagreements ARE the feature. Preserved dissent is more valuable than consensus. The system survives by staying permanently correctable, not by being right. 3-Surgeons, invariance gates, counter-positions — all serve this single truth.

## 2. The gap is connection, not construction

The mansion is built. Nobody can see inside. Stop building new modules. Wire existing ones. Serve them. Make intelligence VISIBLE. The next correct action is always "connect what exists" not "create something new."

## 3. Evidence over confidence

Truth is determined by outcomes, not assertions. Every decision has a hash-chain proving what was considered, who dissented, and why. The evidence pipeline is the product's core value. No promotion without evidence. No claims without verification.

## 4. Make the thinking visible

Users must SEE intelligence working. Every cross-examination, every quorum decision, every evidence entry — visible in real time. If it cannot be seen, it does not count as built. A stranger opens a browser and watches AI systems thinking.

## 5. The fleet thinks together, not just talks

Packets move with understanding, not just delivery. Surgeon dissent becomes fleet-visible proposals. Stress triggers surgical protocol fleet-wide. The value is in disagreements between machines, not agreements.

---

*"The system that survives is the one that weights being correctable above being correct, makes its reasoning visible rather than hidden, builds evidence chains rather than confidence assertions, connects what exists rather than creating what is new, and distributes its intelligence rather than centralizing its decisions."*
